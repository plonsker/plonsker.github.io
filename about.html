Greetings,

Call me Joe.

This is my personal site. As you can probably tell, I have an appreciation for minimalist design.

I am a software developer and technology analyst residing in New York City. I am a member of the Chartcorps team at Chartbeat.

I attended St. John's College in Santa Fe, NM, studying Philosophy and Mathematics through the Great Books Program.

I worked at Berkeley Law for a while before entering the tech scene as a contractor doing data curation and analysis at Apartment List. I then went on to work as a contractor at Google for two years working on operations and expansion for Google Express.

While at Google, I became inspired by the challenges the engineers faced in building new technologies and decided that I should learn to build software applications. During the spring and summer of 2015, I spent nights and weekends teaching myself how to code. Eventually I applied to DBC in order to learn how to build web applications.

My career change took approximately one year. If you are reading this and considering a similar career change, feel free to email me with any questions.

As someone who grew up in San Francisco, I have seen firsthand how the rise in tech has correlated with a rise in social and economic inequality. I believe that many of issues diagnosed within the tech community can be solved through positive action. I hope to help combat this imbalance by supporting and empowering those who have not had the same opportunities I have had.

I enjoy street photography, underground music, weightlifting, music production, and modern philosophy.
