Greetings,

I am Joe, and this is my personal site. It was built with the intent of showing my appreciation of minimalist design.

I am a software engineer and tech support specialist residing in New York City. I work as a tech support representative at Chartbeat.

I attended St. John's College in Santa Fe, NM, studying Philosophy and Mathematics through the Great Books Program.

I worked at Berkeley Law for a while before entering the tech scene as a contractor doing data curation and analysis at Apartment List. I then went on to work as a contractor at Google for two years working on operations and expansion for Google Express.

While at Google, I became inspired by the challenges the engineers faced in building new technologies and decided that I should learn to build software applications. During the spring and summer of 2015, I spent nights and weekends teaching myself how to code. Eventually I applied to DBC in order to learn how to build web applications. My career change took approximately one year. If you are reading this and considering a similar career change and want to get an idea of what my experience was like, feel free to reach out to me via one of the contact forms listed below.

As someone who grew up in San Francisco, I have seen the correlation between the rise in tech and the rise in socio-economic inequality. It is a sensitive topic, but I believe that many of the issues associated with tech culture can be changed through positive action. I plan to do my part by supporting and empowering those who have not had the same opportunities I have had.

I enjoy street photography, underground music, weightlifting, music production, and modern philosophy.
